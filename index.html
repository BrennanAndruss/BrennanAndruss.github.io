A Writing Devlog

Welcome!

This process portfolio illustrates my writing process throughout the quarter sequence assignments as an analogy to developing games. In game development, a devlog is a blog or vlog chronicling the development process of a game. This website can be seen as my devlog for writing. With a series of three sequences, I go in depth on my writing process and demonstrate my growth as a writer.

The process of game development isn't too dissimilar to that of writing. Every great game is born from a great idea, and that comes from dreaming up endless various ideas in a process of discovery and exploration. With that foundation, the first goal is to create a minimum viable product as a prototype of your game. The central game mechanic and game feel are the first thing you have to get down to create an enjoyable game. With that first prototype, others can be brought into the process to test out your game and give their feedback and criticisms of the game. Through a continuous cycle of programming, testing, and refining, all the pieces come together to create the final product for all to enjoy.

For me, the writing process is much of the same. Latching onto that initial idea through brainstorming is what gets you started. In the drafting phase, that idea is developed and clarified to bring focus and depth to your piece. Upon the completion of a rough draft, that when peers come in to review your essay and give their feedback and criticisms. Turning that rough draft into a final submission means taking those insights from your peers and incorporating their feedback into your writing, ensuring the clear message and smooth flow that makes for a great essay.

Sequence Assignments

Sequence 1 was a personal literacy narrative centered around our thoughts and reflections on our literacy growth. I wrote my narrative around my journey in discovering literacy outside the classroom as an outlet for my thoughts.

Sequence 2 was a profile essay highlighting forces of change within our communities. I interviewed and wrote an essay about Patrick Ashman, a fourth-year resident advisor in the Cal Poly dorm rooms.

Sequence 3 was a persuasive essay on an issue impacting our community. I researched and wrote an essay about the affordability of electric vehicles in California, particularly in low-income communities.
